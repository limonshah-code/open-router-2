## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Cordless Dyson Vacuums

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Assess your home’s specific demands: square footage, floor types (hardwood, carpet, tile), presence of pets, and above‑floor cleaning needs (stairs, cars, upholstery). Larger homes benefit from extended runtime and bigger bins, while pet owners prioritize de‑tangling tools and strong suction. Small apartments may prefer lightweight models with decent battery life. Matching a vacuum’s capabilities to your environment ensures efficient, frustration‑free cleaning.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Dyson cordless vacuums range from about $300 for entry‑level models like the V8 Plus to over $900 for premium units such as the V15 Detect Plus or Gen5outsize. Mid‑range V11 models typically cost $400–$600. Non‑Dyson alternatives like the JELLYPIG offer competitive specs for $200–$300. Set a budget based on your must‑have features; remember that higher upfront cost often translates to better durability, filtration, and long‑term value.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Prioritize suction power (measured in Air Watts), battery runtime, and filtration efficiency (look for 99.99% capture at 0.3 microns). Bin size matters for large homes—bigger bins mean fewer interruptions. Power modes (Eco, Auto, Boost) let you balance suction and battery life. Attachments like the Motorbar for all floors, Fluffy Optic for hard surfaces, and Hair Screw for pets address specific cleaning challenges. Weight and ergonomics affect comfort during extended use.

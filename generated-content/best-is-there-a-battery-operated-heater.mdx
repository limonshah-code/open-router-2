### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before purchasing, pinpoint exactly where and how you'll use the heater. For automotive use, prioritize defrosting capabilities, anti-tilt stability for moving vehicles, and compact dashboard fit. For home emergencies during outages, look for longer runtimes, safety certifications, and coverage for small rooms. If you already own tool batteries, consider compatibility to save costs. Assess whether you need supplemental heat for a desk or primary warmth for a tent. Matching the heater's design to your primary scenario ensures optimal performance and avoids overpaying for unnecessary features.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Battery operated heaters range from $30 for basic models with included batteries to over $150 for premium kits with multiple batteries and advanced features. Budget options ($30-$60) often lack batteries or have shorter runtimes, requiring separate purchases. Mid-range ($60-$100) typically includes one or two batteries and solid safety features. Premium ($100-$150+) often come with multiple batteries, higher power output, and USB charging. Factor in long-term costs: proprietary batteries can be expensive, while models compatible with standard tool batteries (18V) may be cheaper if you already own them. Set a budget that balances upfront cost with battery replacement expenses.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Critical specs include battery type (lithium-ion is standard), capacity (mAh), and expected runtime. Heating output measured in watts (W) indicates power—50-100W for personal spaces, 150W+ for small rooms. Coverage area (square meters) tells you how much space it can warm. Temperature settings (usually 2) allow adjustment. Noise level (dB) matters for bedrooms or offices—look for <40dB. Safety features like tip-over shut-off and overheat protection are non-negotiable. For cars, a swiveling head or focused nozzle helps direct heat. USB ports add versatility for charging devices.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Inspect build materials: durable plastics with reinforced joints withstand drops and bumps. For automotive use, ensure the base is weighted or has non-slip pads to stay put on dashboards. Look for certifications like UL or ETL for electrical safety. Battery quality matters—reputable brands use cells with protection circuits. Examine the heater element housing; it should be secure and insulated. Read user reviews focusing on longevity; some heaters fail after a few months. A good warranty (1+ years) indicates manufacturer confidence. Portability features like carrying handles or compact storage are also worth considering for durability during transport.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
While many generic brands offer functional heaters, established brands often provide better support, consistent quality, and safer circuitry. Known brands in power tools (DeWalt, Makita) sometimes have compatible heaters if you use their batteries. For dedicated heaters, research brands with positive long-term reviews. Check for responsive customer service and accessible warranty claims. Be wary of unknown brands with oddly low prices—they may cut corners on battery management systems, risking overheating. A reputable brand invests in safety testing and durable components, reducing the risk of failure or hazards.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your intended storage space—car glove compartment, backpack, or desk drawer. Weight is crucial for frequent transport; under 3 lbs is ideal for travel. Dimensions should allow stowage without bulk. A collapsible or minimal-profile design helps. Carrying handles or grips add convenience. For camping, a smaller footprint fits tents better. Consider how the heater will be positioned: a wide base prevents tipping in vehicles, while a slim profile slides under seats. Balance size with battery capacity—larger batteries extend runtime but increase weight. Choose the smallest unit that meets your power needs for true portability.

## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Gaming Router Under 200

### Find Your Need
Compatibility and use case are crucial when selecting a gaming router. Consider your gaming platforms—PC, PlayStation, Xbox, or Nintendo Switch—and ensure the router supports wired connections for low latency. If you stream 4K content or have a smart home, look for higher bandwidth and device capacity. Assess your internet plan speed; a router with 2.5G or 10G ports is beneficial for gigabit plans. For large homes, prioritize range and mesh compatibility. Identify if you need VPN support, parental controls, or specific QoS features for gaming traffic.

### Budget
Under $200 offers a sweet spot for advanced features without overspending. Budget options around $80-$120 provide solid WiFi 6 or entry WiFi 7 with basic gaming features. Mid-range $120-$170 includes tri-band WiFi 6E or WiFi 7 with more ports and acceleration. Premium picks near $200 offer quad-band WiFi 7, 10G ports, and extensive customization. Set a realistic budget based on your needs; casual gamers can opt for budget models, while competitive players may invest in premium for every edge.

### Key Features
Focus on WiFi standards: WiFi 6 for good performance, WiFi 6E for 6 GHz band, or WiFi 7 for ultimate speed and MLO. Ports matter—2.5G or 10G ports future-proof for high-speed internet. Look for dedicated gaming ports, QoS (Quality of Service) for traffic prioritization, and game acceleration technologies. Security features like WPA3, firewall, and parental controls add value. MIMO streams (e.g., 6-stream, 12-stream) affect capacity and range. Ensure the router supports your modem and has enough Ethernet ports for wired devices.

### Quality & Durability
Examine build materials—metal housings dissipate heat better than plastic, ensuring longevity during intense gaming sessions. Check antenna design; external, adjustable antennas often provide better range. Look for routers with heat sinks or ventilation to prevent throttling. Read user reviews about reliability over time. Brands with warranties offer peace of mind. A sturdy build reduces wear and tear, especially if placed in high-traffic areas or near consoles with high power draw.

### Brand Reputation
Established manufacturers like TP-Link, ASUS, NETGEAR, and GL.iNet have proven track records in networking hardware. They offer regular firmware updates, better customer support, and robust security patches. Premium brands invest in gaming-specific optimizations and software ecosystems like DumaOS or ASUS WRT. Research brand history for router reliability and innovation. Avoid unknown brands that may cut corners on components or software support, leading to instability or security risks.

### Portability/Size
Consider router size if space is limited—some gaming routers are large with prominent antennas. Measure your intended placement area; wall-mount options can save space. Smaller models like the HYPEREV booster are portable but lack full router capabilities. For frequent moves or small apartments, compact designs are advantageous. However, larger routers often have better cooling and antenna performance, so balance size with performance needs.

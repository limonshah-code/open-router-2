<h2>What to Look for Before Buying the Best Ge Counter Depth French Door Refrigerator Slate</h2>

<h3>### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations</h3>
<p>Start by measuring your kitchen cutout precisely—width, depth, and height—including necessary clearance for ventilation and door swing. A counter-depth fridge should align flush or nearly flush with your counters. Consider your household size and grocery habits. A family of four or more will need 22+ cu. ft., while a couple might thrive with 18-20 cu. ft. Think about your cooking style: do you entertain often and need specialized storage like a temperature-controlled drawer? Do you buy lots of fresh produce, making advanced humidity control (like TwinChill) valuable? Align the fridge’s features with your actual daily routines.</p>

<h3>### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations</h3>
